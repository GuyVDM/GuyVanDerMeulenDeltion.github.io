What i worked on this project:

- Interaction

- Tutorial

- Sound

- Movement

- Unity Animations

- Puzzles

This is "Project Blackfired" and has been my first look into VR development, in this project i had the role of Lead Developer.

This project is a VR Escape Room project, with this projects goal to explore throughout the level, do puzzles, progress, and reach the end as fast as possible.

Ofcourse at the start of such a project where you have no insight about what to do you start to look into the basics,

and i quickly found out that the best approach to a workflow was to build things that were very modulair and easy to use.

But luckily enough for me, was the fact that VR development and the approach that was needed wasn't very alien to me.

So what i did first was what and how the player can interact with an object. I decided to create an base Abstract Class that would have

one base function "Interact", and that would always be activated once a player grabbed/interacted with an interactable,

and would handle whatever what was in the function.

Even though our game was supposed to be small and compact, we still wanted to make sure that even new players got a hint of where to go.

This came up to be the next challenge, and after a while of brainstorming of how & what, i decided to create a region loading system.

This system would have a function that'd make it so that when the player progressed throughout the level, another region would be loaded,

and the other regions would unload, this was made with the reason so that the player would experience minimal backtracking.

If the player were to attempt to backtrack, he would be faced with a fading black screen when he goes out of bounds,

and with the attempt of the player teleporting into a room where he has not yet progress to, he's met with the fact that he cannot go there.
